Mead Recipe

Description:

The Nordic Mead is an alcoholic beverage with a concentration that varies from 4% to 18%
and is obtained from the fermentation of a mixture of water and honey.

It is considered to be the first of the alcoholic beverages that the human being consumed and is believed to be the precursor of beer.

Ingredients:

  • 1kg Honey
  • 2,5L Water
  • 3g brewer's yeast powder
  • Pot
  • Sanitizer
  • Thermometer for hot drinks
  • Fermenter

Steps:

  1. Disinfect all the materials that you are going to use with 96º ethyl alcohol or with food bleach.
  2. Put a pot with half the water on the fire until it reaches a temperature of approx. 50 degrees centigrade.
  3. Add the honey to the pot along with the hot water while stirring. TIP: Do not add the honey to more than 50º, otherwise it will lose its properties.
  4. Add the liquid to the container you are going to use for the fermentation, add the rest of the water until reaching a temperature between 25º and 45º and mix again.
  5. Separate a glass of the liquid and add the brewer's yeast or the fresh baker's yeast broken up with your fingers.
  6. Let the yeast act without stirring for 10 minutes.
  7. Shake the mixture in the glass and let stand 5 more minutes.
  8. Add the liquid from the glass to the fermenter and close.
  9. Leave to ferment between 15 days and a month in a place with a temperature between 10º and 20ºC and out of reach of the sun's rays (there may be light, but do not give it direct sunlight).
